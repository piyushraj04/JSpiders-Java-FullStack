Description

Samosa is a popular Indian snack consisting of a crispy pastry shell filled with a spicy mixture of mashed potatoes, peas, and aromatic spices. It's deep-fried to golden perfection and is a favorite tea-time treat.

üç¥ Ingredients
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 4 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1/2 tsp carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp amchur (dry mango powder)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for deep frying
  • For Filling:
  • 2 cups boiled and mashed potatoes
  • 1/2 cup boiled peas
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tbsp chopped coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste
üßë‚Äçüç≥ Method
  1. In a mixing bowl, combine flour, carom seeds, turmeric, and salt. Add oil and rub it into the flour until it resembles breadcrumbs.
  2. Gradually add water to form a firm dough. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  3. For the filling, heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds, chopped onions, and saut√© until golden.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Saut√© for a minute.
  5. Add boiled potatoes, peas, and all the spices. Mix well and cook for 5 minutes. Add chopped coriander leaves and remove from heat.
  6. Divide the dough into equal portions. Roll each portion into a ball and flatten it into an oval disc.
  7. Cut the disc in half to form two semi-circles. Shape each semi-circle into a cone.
  8. Fill the cone with the prepared filling and seal the edges to form a triangular shape.
  9. Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Fry the samosas on medium heat until golden brown and crispy.
  10. Drain on paper towels and serve hot with mint chutney or tamarind sauce.
